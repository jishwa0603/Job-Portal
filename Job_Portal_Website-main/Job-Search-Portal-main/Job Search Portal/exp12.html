About Samsung

Company Overview

Samsung is a global conglomerate known for its significant contributions to various industries, including electronics, information technology, and heavy industries. Founded in 1938 by Lee Byung-chul in Su-dong, Korea, Samsung started as a trading company and has grown into one of the world's largest technology companies. As of 2023, Samsung's market valuation exceeds $500 billion, making it one of the most valuable brands globally. The company is headquartered in Seoul, South Korea, and operates across multiple sectors, with a primary focus on electronics and information technology.

Industry

Consumer Electronics, Information Technology, and Heavy Industries

Company Size

Samsung operates in over 80 countries with more than 320,000 employees working in 400 offices worldwide.

Products, Services, and Operations

Samsung offers a diverse range of products and services, including:

  • Consumer Electronics: Smartphones, tablets, televisions, and home appliances.
  • Semiconductors: Memory chips, processors, and storage solutions.
  • Display Technology: OLED and QLED panels for various devices.
  • IT & Mobile Communications: Networking equipment, laptops, and wearables.
  • Home Appliances: Refrigerators, washing machines, air conditioners.
  • Heavy Industries: Shipbuilding, construction, and engineering.

Popular Products

  • Samsung Galaxy Series: Smartphones and tablets with advanced features.
  • Samsung QLED TVs: High-definition televisions with superior picture quality.
  • Samsung SSDs: High-speed solid-state drives for storage solutions.
  • Samsung Home Appliances: Innovative and energy-efficient home appliances.

Acquisitions

Samsung has strategically expanded its capabilities and market reach through several acquisitions. Notable acquisitions include:

  • Acquisition of Harman International: In 2017, Samsung acquired Harman International, a leading provider of connected car technology, for $8 billion. This acquisition enhanced Samsung's presence in the automotive electronics sector.
  • Acquisition of SmartThings: In 2014, Samsung acquired SmartThings, a leading IoT platform, for $200 million. This acquisition bolstered Samsung's smart home and IoT capabilities.
