About IBM

Company Overview

IBM (International Business Machines Corporation) is a global technology company that provides hardware, software, cloud-based services, and cognitive computing. Founded in 1911 by Charles Ranlett Flint, IBM has grown into one of the world's largest and most influential tech companies. With a market valuation of approximately $120 billion as of 2023, IBM continues to be a leader in technological innovation and business solutions.

Industry

Information Technology and Services

Company Size

IBM operates in over 170 countries with more than 345,000 employees globally, making it one of the largest technology employers in the world.

Products, Services, and Operations

  • Cloud Computing: IBM Cloud, hybrid cloud solutions, and cloud management services.
  • Cognitive Computing: IBM Watson, AI, and machine learning services.
  • IT Infrastructure: Servers, storage, and mainframes.
  • Software: Enterprise software, analytics, and cybersecurity.
  • Consulting Services: Business consulting, IT consulting, and technology consulting.

Acquisitions

  • Cloud Computing: In 2019, IBM acquired Red Hat, a leading provider of open-source software solutions, for $34 billion. This acquisition significantly strengthened IBM's hybrid cloud capabilities.
  • Cybersecurity: The acquisition of Resilient Systems in 2016 enabled IBM to enhance its cybersecurity services, particularly in incident response.
  • Data and AI: IBM's acquisition of The Weather Company in 2016 helped bolster its data analytics and AI capabilities, particularly in predictive analytics.

These acquisitions have significantly contributed to IBM's growth, with the company generating an annual revenue of over $57 billion.
