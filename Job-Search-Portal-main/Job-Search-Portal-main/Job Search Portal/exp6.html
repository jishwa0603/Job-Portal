About Meta Platforms, Inc.

Company Overview

Meta Platforms, Inc., previously known as Facebook, Inc., is an American multinational technology conglomerate headquartered in Menlo Park, California. Founded in 2004 by Mark Zuckerberg, along with his college roommates Eduardo Saverin, Andrew McCollum, Dustin Moskovitz, and Chris Hughes, Meta has grown to become one of the most influential tech companies globally. The company rebranded to Meta in October 2021 to emphasize its focus on building the metaverse. As of 2024, Meta's market valuation is approximately $610 billion.

Industry

Industry: Technology and Social Media

Company Size

Meta employs around 69,329 people globally as of March 2024. The company operates across various continents, with major offices in North America, Europe, and Asia.

Products, Services, and Operations

  • Facebook: The original social networking platform.
  • Instagram: A photo and video sharing social network.
  • WhatsApp: A widely used messaging app.
  • Messenger: A messaging platform integrated with Facebook.
  • Meta Quest: Virtual reality headsets and software.
  • Threads: A social media platform for text-based posts.
  • Reality Labs: Focused on augmented reality (AR) and virtual reality (VR) technologies.
